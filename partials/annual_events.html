Sastha Mandala Pooja

Lord Sasta, also popularly known as Lord Ayyappa is child of Shiva and Vishnu in female form. Sastha having a name of Annadhana Prabhu, VEDA Circle begins the new year with aplomb celebrating with pooja to Lord Ayyappa and annadhana

Maha Sivaratri Pooja

Maha Shivaratri is celebrated on the Krishna Paksha Chaturdashi of the month of Maasi which falls in February or March. Of the twelve Shivaratris in the year, the Maha Shivarathri is the most holy. On this night abishekam is offered to Lord Shiva throughout the night and he is celebrated by offerings of Bilva leaves.

Sri Rama Navami Pooja

Rama Navami celebrates the birth of Lord Rama. Rama Navami falls in the Shukla Paksha on the Navami, the ninth day of the month of Chaitra in the Hindu calendar and marks the end of the nine-day Vasanta Navaratri celebrations. Rama Navami is celebrated with pooja and the prasad of neer-mor (buttermilk) and panagam.

Sankara, Ramanuja & Madhva Jayanthis

The life and the work of the three important religious philosophers of our Santhana Dharma by celebrating the day of their Jayanti. Ashotothra Namavallis in praise of the gurus are chanted followed by vedic chanting. In celebrating the Jayantis, the VEDA Circle keeps up our tradition of respecting our gurus.

Hanuman Jayanthi

Hanuman Jayanthi is observed either in Chaitra (usually on the day of Chaitra Pournimaa) or in Dhanu (called Margazhi in Tamil). Hanuman is the symbol of strength and energy. Chanting of Hanuman Chalisa, it removes all obstacles in life.

Upakarma, Gayatri Japam and Homam

The learning of the Vedas begins in the month of Aavani with the Upakarma. On this day Homams are held and Poonal, the sacred thread, is changed. This day is also auspicious as the Brahmins offer libations of water to their ancestors to whom they owe their birth and to the great Rishis to whom they are highly indebted for spiritual knowledge and the Vedas themselves. Every year, on the day of Gayatri Japam, the Gayatri mantra is chanted 1008 times, as a prayaschitta (atonement) for faults that one has knowlingly and unknowingly committed.

Krishna Janmashtami Pooja

Krishna Janmashtami, also known Gokulashtami, is festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Vishnu. It is observed on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) in Shraavana maasa and which overlaps with August and September. It is celeberated with pooja followed by devotional singing through the midnight.

Guru Poornima Pooja and VEDA Circle Day

The Full Moon Day in the month of Ashadha (July-August) is known as Guru Purnima.This was the day, when Krishna-Dwaipayana Vyasa was born to sage Parashara and Satyavati, thus this day is also celebrated as Vyasa Poornima. This day is celebrated in honour of the great sage Vyasa, who is seen as one of the greatest gurus in ancient Hindu traditions and a symbol of the Guru-shishya tradition. This day is observed by offering puja to one’s guru and the guru parampara.
VEDA Circle celeberates Guru Poornima day as commemmoration anniversary of the organization. The main purpose and vision of VEDA Circle is to propagation of Veda and Vedic tradition, and our Honorable Guru Pandit Sivaramakrishnan living by that vision and executing that vision tirelessly in our community by teaching Veda to all in our community and beyond. So to live by example, VEDA Circle honors our Guru on this day as part of the annual event.

Vinayaka Chaturthi Pooja

Vinayaka Chaturthi is celebrated on the fourth day after new moon in the month of aavani. On this day Ganesh idols made of clay are worshipped in all homes. Ganesh is decorated with garlands and worshipped with durva grass (arukampul). Modak, ladoo and other dishes are offered to Ganesha to remove all obstacles and bless the devotee with prosperity.

Navaratri Deepa Pooja & Chandi Homam

The Worship of Goddess Chandi highlights the importance of feminine energy. Chandi Homam is one of the most popular Homas during Navarathri. The core of the ceremony involves a recitation of 700 verses of the Durga Saptashati. With the recitation of each verse, offerings are made in the sacrificial fire invoking the Goddess. The performance of Chandi Homa, frees one of curses, obstacles and blesses them with a lasting health, wealth and prosperity.

Arupadai Veedu Utsavam

Skanda Sashti Kavacham  (கந்த சஷ்டி கவசம்) was composed by Sri Devaraya Swamigal in 19th century. Devotees attest that regular chanting of this kavacham resolves all the predicaments of life and provides enormous mental strength. Sri Devaraya Swamigal asserts in his composition.
VEDA Circle began organising a Arupadai Veedu Sashti Utsavam at 6 different locations in USA similar to 6 veedu at Tamilnadu and celeberate Sashti Utsavam and Soora Samharam for the welfare of community.

Karthigai Deepa Pooja

VEDA Circle celeberates Karthigai Deepam festival with Deepa Pooja. Kartigai Deepam is celebrated in the Tamil month of Karthigai. It is celebrated on the full moon day of the Karthigai month(November-December) which coincides with Karthika star. One must light deepam every evening in the entrance and do the pooja on all the 30 days of this month. This festival is celebrated in a special manner in Thiruvannamalai. Lord Shiva takes the form of a jyothi (light of fire) on the hill of Thiruvannamalai. Therefore, this festival is also known as Annamalai Deepam. Here, a special deepam is lighted on the zenith of the hill and it is believed that Lord Shiva's jothi will be visible on this day. Lord Muruga took the form of six babies in a lake called "Saravana Poigai". On this day, all his six forms were united by Parvathi (his mother) and this way, he had six faces. Special poojas are performed to Lord Muruga.

Annual Mahanyasa Poorvaka Ekadasa Rudra Parayanam

VEDA Circle concludes every year in a spiritual way by performing Mahanyasa Poorvaka Ekadasa Rudra Parayanam during last week of Decemenber.
